Reimagining the Pediatric Waiting Areas

Leveraging Augmented Reality for Enhanced Child Well-being in Hospital Waiting Areas

Overview

Problem

Waiting for medical appointments in hospitals can cause stress in children and negatively affect their medical outcomes.

Literature Research Insights

Emotional Impact of Waiting Areas: Traditional waiting rooms often heighten pediatric patients' stress and anxiety. These spaces can make children feel confined due to a lack of stimulating and engaging elements.

Importance of Play Research underscores the essential role of play in reducing stress and enhancing emotional health. Engaging in play helps distract children from medical anxieties and shortens perceived wait times.

Nature as a Calming Element: Visual elements of nature are proven to soothe and relax children. Incorporating natural elements into waiting areas can create a tranquil and comforting environment.

Question

How can we integrate nature-based elements in the design of pediatric healthcare waiting areas to enhance the children's well-being?

Pre-Design: Exploratory Design

Open Processing

Idea Generation

Hospital Divider:Waiting areas in hospitals typically use dividers for physical separation. By reimagining these dividers with augmented reality (AR) offers a way to transform these physical barriers into engaging, interactive experiences, thus reducing the stress and changing the perception of the waiting area from a place of isolation to one of engagement and interaction.

Hygiene and Safety:In the context of a healthcare setting, maintaining hygiene is paramount, especially in pediatric areas. Traditional play areas and shared toys can pose risks for germ transmission. An AR-enhanced divider utilizing hand-tracking eliminates the need for physical contact, ensuring that children can play and interact in a safe, germ-free environment.

Focused Interaction and Distraction: The AR divider can serve as a focal point for interaction that captivates children's attention. By integrating nature themes and playful elements into the divider's design, it acts as a powerful tool for distraction, helping to alleviate anxiety and fear associated with medical visits. This focused interaction not only entertains but also comforts the children during potentially long wait times.
